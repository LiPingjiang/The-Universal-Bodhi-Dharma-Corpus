# Edgerton《佛教混合梵語辭典》

**書名**：Buddhist Hybrid Sanskrit Grammar and Dictionary（佛教混合梵語語法與辭典）  
**作者**：Franklin Edgerton（富蘭克林·艾吉頓）  
**年代**：1953年出版（耶魯大學出版社）  
**規模**：語法一卷，辭典兩卷（約一千五百頁），收錄詞條逾一萬五千條

---

## 結構與詞條範圍

本書為佛教混合梵語（Buddhist Hybrid Sanskrit，簡稱BHS）之權威研究工具，包含：
- **語法卷**：詳述佛教混合梵語之音韻、構詞、詞形變化、句法特徵
- **辭典卷**：收錄BHS文獻中出現之詞彙，附詳細語法標註

收錄文獻範圍：
- 梵語大乘經典（如《法華經》、《華嚴經》、《般若經》各種版本）
- 梵語律藏（如《根本說一切有部毗奈耶》）
- 梵語論書（如《大乘莊嚴經論》、《入菩薩行論》）
- 梵語本生故事（如《本生鬘》Jātakamālā）
- 其他混合梵語佛教文獻

每詞條標註詞性、語源、語法變化、出處文獻及對應巴利語形式。

## 代表性內容摘錄

### 詞條「bodhisattva」

> BODHISATTVA, m. (also rarely nt. in the sense of 'condition, career of a B.S.', = next; in the latter use Skt. Lex. and Mvy, but not the 'regular' Skt. text; in Pali only as m. in the sense of person), one who is destined for enlightenment, or in the process of achieving it; regularly as a Buddhist technical term applied to a future Buddha, especially the historical Buddha in his previous existences; but also applied to the 'mahāsattvas' (great beings) of the Mahāyāna...

> The BHS word is a hybrid formation, the second element corresponding to Pali satta (Skt. sattva), while the first is the Sanskrit form. The word is essentially a BHS term, though it occurs in standard Sanskrit in the sense of the name of the historical Buddha in former lives.

### 詞條「dhāraṇī」

> DHĀRAṆĪ, f. (also °ni, see §§ 10.23, 22.32; in Skt. recorded as a relatively rare word, 'memory' or 'the power of memory', a relatively unimportant use; in BHS clearly a magic formula, 'spell' or 'charm', sometimes, in the canonical texts, in a less magical and more edifying sense, a 'summary' or 'compendium' of doctrine, so that it 'retains' the teaching; but in the popular texts and in the Tantras, clearly a magical spell; it is significant that the word is far more common in BHS than in standard Sanskrit)...

### 詞條「karma」

> KARMA, nt. (also m. in comp., see § 8.36; = Pali kamma), deed, act, action; in BHS as in Pali and Skt., especially in the ethical sense of an act which has consequences (karma-vipāka) for the doer. The term is one of the most fundamental in Buddhist philosophy. In the general Buddhist view, all actions of body, speech, and mind (kāya-karma, vāṅ-karma, mano-karma) produce corresponding results, and thus determine the course of saṃsāra (rebirth). The doctrine of karma is central to Buddhist ethics and soteriology...
